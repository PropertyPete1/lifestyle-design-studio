## Sunday (2026-08-02)
**Topic:** market_now | **Format:** bold_statement | **Ending:** question | **Words:** 130

Pricing right in an uncertain market beats chasing it.

When the market shifts, agents split into two groups. One group reprices constantly, chasing whatever they think the market wants. The other prices thoughtfully once and lets the market come to them. The second group closes more deals and spends less time on price negotiations that go nowhere. Pricing right means having a real conversation with your seller about what their home is actually worth based on condition, location, and comparable sales. Not emotion. Not hope. Then you defend that price when offers come in. Buyers know the difference between a realistic price and wishful thinking. So do other agents.

What's more valuable to your sellers. A price that makes them feel good or a strategy that actually sells the house?
